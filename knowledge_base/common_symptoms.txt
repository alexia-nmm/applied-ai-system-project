Vomiting once is usually not an emergency, but repeated vomiting, blood in vomit, or vomiting with lethargy requires immediate vet attention.
Diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, or diarrhea with blood, warrants a vet visit.
Lethargy combined with loss of appetite for more than 24 hours is a warning sign in both dogs and cats.
Excessive thirst and urination can indicate diabetes or kidney disease in both dogs and cats.
Limping that persists more than a day, or any limb that appears swollen or deformed, needs veterinary evaluation.
Coughing in cats is not normal and may indicate asthma, heart disease, or respiratory infection.
A cat that stops using the litter box may have a urinary tract infection, kidney stones, or a behavioral issue.
